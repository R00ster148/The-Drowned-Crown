THE DROWNED CROWNThree Acts of the Last Tide

The old world is six fathoms under and sinking further. What's left clings to drowned mountaintops and the bones of cathedrals. You hold one such foothold. The sea is patient. You are not.

Three generations ago the Sundering Flood swallowed every kingdom that mattered. Now the Salt Crown — your crown — is one of a dozen scattered city-states fighting over the last dry stone, while something old and enormous dreams beneath the waves and slowly, patiently, pulls the world back down to it.

Briefing
How to Hold the Tide

The Map. Each island is a territory. Click one you own to manage it, or one you don't to consider attacking it. Sea lanes connect islands — you can only move fleets and strike along connected lanes.

Resources. Salvage builds things. Provisions feed your fleets — let them run dry and ships desert. Lumen is the light that holds the Deep back and recruits companions. Influence buys loyalty and diplomacy.

The Deep Pressure. Watch the meter top-right. It rises every turn. Lighthouses and Lumen spending push it back. If it reaches 100, the tide takes everything — that's how you lose.

Companions. Assign them as Fleet Commanders or Governors. They level up, carry personal questlines that unfold as you play, and can be lost for good in a bad battle.

Winning. Stabilize each Act by breaking the rival factions and claiming enough territory, then survive to light the Last Lighthouse in the Maw. Three Acts. One crown. Good luck.
